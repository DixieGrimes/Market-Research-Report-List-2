<p><p>The polysilicon for electronics market is categorized into three grades based on purity and application. Grade I polysilicon, known as electronic-grade polysilicon, has the highest purity, suitable for semiconductor manufacturing. Grade II, or solar-grade polysilicon, offers moderate purity, primarily used for photovoltaic cells in solar panels. Grade III polysilicon has lower purity levels, generally utilized for lower-grade applications or non-critical electronic components. These distinctions are essential for meeting the specific requirements of various electronic and renewable energy technologies.</p></p>
