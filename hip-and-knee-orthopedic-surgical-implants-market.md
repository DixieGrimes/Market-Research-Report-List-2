<p><p>The hip and knee orthopedic surgical implants market consists of three primary types: ceramic, metal, and polymer implants. Ceramic implants offer excellent biocompatibility and wear resistance, making them suitable for long-lasting joint replacements. Metal implants, typically made from titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, provide strength and durability, ideal for high-load applications. Polymer implants, often used as bearing surfaces or in combination with metal components, are lightweight and flexible, reducing friction and wear in joint movements. Each type addresses specific patient needs and surgical requirements.</p></p>
